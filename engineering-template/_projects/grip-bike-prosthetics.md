## GRiP Bike Prosthetics (Full Exhibit)

This project focused on restoring mobility through custom bike prosthetic solutions for patients who wanted to ride again.

## Scope and Leadership

- Led a team of **8 students** through concept, CAD iteration, and build planning
- Helped deliver **3 bike prosthetic solutions** for individual recipients
- Mentored junior students through design reviews, part-fit decisions, and implementation tradeoffs
- Coordinated technical progress with patient-centered usability goals

## Technical Contribution

I owned key CAD work for functional geometry, fit, and manufacturable part interfaces, while guiding integration across the team’s subcomponents. The workflow emphasized rapid iteration, practical tolerances, and build-ready outputs that could be adjusted to individual rider needs.

## Clinical/Community Impact

The most important outcome was real use: patients receiving their devices and getting back on bikes. The project combined engineering execution with longitudinal mentorship and delivery accountability.
