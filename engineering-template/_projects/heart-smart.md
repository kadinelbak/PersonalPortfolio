## Heart Smart (Designathon Project)

Heart Smart is an educational CHF model designed to help patients physically and visually understand congestive heart failure mechanisms so they can better follow prevention and treatment guidance.

## Clinical Need

Congestive Heart Failure (CHF) has high hospitalization burden and is difficult for many patients to conceptualize from verbal explanation alone. The project targets the communication gap between clinician instructions and patient behavior change by making disease dynamics tangible.

## Designed Solution

The team built a compact, interactive liquid-loop model of the heart and circulation for use during office conversations:

- A hand-driven squeeze pump represents cardiac pumping effort
- A valve setting toggles between lower-resistance (healthy) and higher-resistance (CHF-like) states
- One-way flow paths and visible fluid movement simulate circulation
- Flow diversion into lung/leg compartments demonstrates pooling/swelling effects
- The model is resettable between patient demonstrations

This intentionally prioritizes comprehension and engagement over full physiologic fidelity, so patients can clearly feel and see what worsening CHF means.

## Design Requirements Covered

- Easy for non-technical patients to understand
- Visually realistic enough to improve recall during counseling
- Compact for clinic exam-room use
- Buildable with accessible materials for lower-cost deployment

## Outcome & Translation

Heart Smart was submitted to DTE Designathon 2024 and recognized as a winning design. The concept supports patient education, adherence conversations, and shared decision-making in early CHF management.
