## NeuroVac (Junior Design)

Team NeuroVac project focused on a non-invasive pressure modulation concept for patients with Sunken Skin Flap Syndrome (SSFS) after decompressive craniectomy.

## Clinical Problem

After decompressive craniectomy, atmospheric pressure can exceed intracranial pressure at the skull defect, causing SSFS and neurological decline (e.g., severe headaches, reduced mobility, seizures, cognitive symptoms). Existing temporary options can be uncomfortable, limited in mobility, and difficult to individualize.

## Proposed Device Concept

NeuroVac is a pressure and depth modulating vacuum-chamber system intended to restore safer pressure balance over the affected area while improving portability and comfort.

- Flexible film attachment and sealed chamber over the defect region
- Depth sensor + pressure sensor for real-time feedback
- Pump with two one-way valves for controlled air in/out flow
- Gradual pressure adjustment logic to reduce over-correction risk
- Portable form factor intended for longer-term wear and clinical monitoring

## Design Constraints Highlighted in the Report

- Safety: precise pressure modulation and biocompatible materials
- Functionality: secure adhesive attachment, non-porous chamber, reliable sensing
- Comfort and mobility: compact/lightweight external system
- Regulatory readiness: concept framed with class III pathway and staged testing
